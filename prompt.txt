STEP 2 — WHAT YOU ARE BUILDING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

LifeScore is India's first holistic life benchmarking and elevation platform. It shows Indian working professionals exactly where they stand — financially, professionally, and physically — compared to real peers of the same age, city, income bracket, and industry. Then it tells them exactly what to do about it.

THE THREE PILLARS:

1. FINANCIAL SCORE (0-100)
   - Benchmarks savings rate, insurance coverage, EMI burden, emergency fund, investment allocation
   - Shows percentile rank vs. peers (same age + city + income cohort)
   - Surfaces red flags: no term insurance, negative real returns on FDs, under-saving vs. cohort
   - One actionable move: the single highest-leverage financial action this week

2. CAREER SCORE (0-100)
   - Benchmarks salary vs. market rate (role + city + years of experience)
   - Shows career velocity: promotion pace vs. cohort
   - Surfaces skills gap: what the top 25% at the next level have that the user doesn't
   - Job switch signal: is now the right time to move?
   - One actionable move: negotiate, upskill, or switch — ranked by impact

3. HEALTH SCORE (0-100)
   - Benchmarks sleep, exercise frequency, BMI, stress level
   - Shows health percentile vs. age/gender/city cohort
   - ActiveMatch: recommends the best gym, sport, or activity based on:
     * Financial Fit: derived from Financial Score — never recommends spending that hurts savings
     * Health Fit: maps health signals (BMI, sleep, stress) to suitable activities
     * Geography: nearest venues within user's city, pincode-aware
     * Schedule Fit: recommends timing based on work patterns and sleep data

LIFESCORE ELEVATE — THE ACTION LAYER:
   - Weekly brief: one financial action + one career action delivered every Monday
   - Action Ranker: every possible action scored by Impact ÷ Effort × Urgency
   - Dual-Elevation Moments: actions that improve two pillars simultaneously
   - Example: "Your salary gap costs you more than your spending. Negotiate first, then SIP."
   - Loss-frame language: "You are losing ₹18,000/year" not "You could earn more"
   - Social proof: "People like you who did X saw Y improvement in Z weeks"
